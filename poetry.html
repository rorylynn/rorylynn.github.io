Poetry

October

Plum colored hands shudder and whine

Raw from hollowing out a space

In the snow

Stomach vibrating inside

Clawing its’ way up my throat

I swallow it back down

A crow crawls in stained snowflakes

Its leg contorted inward

The sun sets and watches it die

A Light On

Theirs a light on in the building down the street

The sun is missed

There’s a light on in my bathroom

The floor is cold

Lie

Their’s a flicker on in your room

It gets closer

Gone

There's a light on

Their was

Rot

I lie in bed, watching car lights reflect on the wall. Wallowing in the depths of my sadness. I begin to scratch at my scalp, trying to rid my brain of it. I pick and pick, until a chunk of brain falls out, landing bloody on the white sheets. I continue to pull my brain apart, using both hands. Strings of red-stained blonde hair and skull fragments cling to my nails. The desperation to be free takes hold of me. Soon there will be no brain left. No world. Peace.

Solitude

Winter trees

Alone in the forest

Still hands

Trees, barren and frozen in mid-January. Snow piles up on brittle branches until they snap and collide with the ground. After the collision, there is silence once again. Dragging deep into the woods, cushioned in the snow lies a pair of wrinkled, worker’s hands, connected to the body of a man, whose last crooked smile is pressed into the snow. He is slowly covered by mounds of snow, blending into the earth.

Haiku

The bird fails to fly

Singing to the air,

The man trudges along.

There

The streetlights turn everything yellow and I am alone.

Everyone I pass is faceless and barely there.

Their skin is flat and one-dimensional

They never had noses or mouths or eyes

They’ve always been like this

They walk by me

Every day

But the minute they pass

I forget they were ever there

I think they never were

I see myself in the window of a hair salon

But when I look again,

I’m not there.

I think I never was.

Everything is blurred and the streetlights

Stretch themselves inside the concrete,

Disappearing underneath me.

Everything is still yellow.

There is a thumping underneath the street.

It gets louder as I lean down,

But when my ear touches the ground

There is nothing there.

It starts up again when I rise

I can never catch it.
